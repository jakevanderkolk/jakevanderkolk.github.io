Course Description and Objectives

This course introduces you to the study of literature in German. That means first that it will introduce you to German literature – to important works of poetry, prose, and drama from the main periods of literary history. Second, the course will introduce you to the study of those works – to the methods of analysis used in literary criticism.

Students will read 20-25 pages in German per week, and show their knowledge of the text and develop their analytic skills through daily read response activities (Hausaufgaben, see below). At the end of the each module, students will write a small essay that demonstrates their ability to interpret a work with the vocabulary and concepts learned for that genre. I expect students to have developed grammatical proficiency through their previous courses, but I also understand that language learning does not end once the study of literature begins. Be prepared to complete grammar-review assignments tailored to the particular errors that surface in your writing. Finally, regular attendance and active participation are essential to success in the course, so always come prepared and turn in your work on time.

This course complies with the Wabash College Academic Credit Policy stated in the Academic Bulletin. In addition to regular class meetings, direct instruction takes place in this course through faculty office hours, online journal submissions, individual presentations and performances of plays, and self-guided cultural enrichment activities.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to
  • read primary literature in German,
  • analyze form and content with the tools and terminology of literary criticism,
  • recognize some of the major authors, genres, and periods in German literature,
  • discuss literary works with the necessary vocabulary,
  • present his ideas in precise written German.
For a complete description, see this course's unabridged Student Learning Outcomes document.

Participation (die Beteiligung, 15%)

For each class period, you will receive 0, 1, or 2 points for your participation, depending on the following criteria.

  • Presence. Let Dr. van der Kolk know if you will not be attending a class session. If you miss a class session, you will receive 0 points for that session. However, you can still get 2 points by submitting an "asynchronous" attendance assignment. Follow the directions in this form to do so. You may do this for any absence, excused or unexcused. For unexcused absences, you must submit the assignment within 48 hours of your missed session. For college-sanctioned excused absences, you must submit the assignment within 48 hours of the end of the event.
    • Tardy. If you are more than 5 minutes, your maximum score for the day is reduced by 1 point.

Criteria. For each class session you attend in person or otherwise, you must do all of the following to get all available points. There is no partial credit.

  • Engagement. You involve yourself in all class meetings, including group and whole-class activities, and make a good faith effort to express yourself in German. If you are in a distant session, you must have your video on if you are on-campus (but stay muted unless you are speaking). However:
    • Linguistic Correctness. You are not expected to speak perfectly, just to try your best as often as you can with the language you have learned!
  • Preparation. You come to class ready and willing to practice the vocabulary, grammar, and topics touched on in your homework and previous class sessions. You have all necessary materials (books, worksheets, notes, etc.) at hand.
  • Cooperation. You support and encourage your fellow students. Be an asset to others' learning, and they will be an asset to you.

Daily Homework (Tägliche Hausaufgaben, 20%)

Prior to each class day, you will complete homework responding to this course's readings. Your daily contribution helps shape class discussions, identify problematic grammar and vocabulary, and develop ideas for your essays. Your overall grade will be the percentage of assignments passed.

Criteria. To get credit for a day's Hausaufgaben, you must achieve all of the following. There is no partial credit:
  • On Time. You submit your daily entry by 11:00 am on the day of class.
  • In German. Unless stated otherwise, you write your journal entry completely in German.
  • Completion. You include all necessary parts as listed in the directions in Canvas.
  • Good-faith effort. You you put a genuine effort into this assignment, and it reflects a genuine effort on your part to engage with the reading and develop your ideas. Think of this as practice for your essay writing!

Tests (die Prüfungen, 40%; 10% each) and Presentations (die Referate, 20%; 5% each)

For each unit (Kurzgeschichten, Gedichte, Dramen und Romane), you will write and present on a topic related to our readings. Depending on the form of assessment chosen by the whole class for each unit, each test and presentation will be one of the following.

  • Option A: The Conventional Option
    • Prüfung: A conventional essay written outside of class on a topic of your choice.
    • Referat: A preview of your essay you present to the whole class when you submit your Volle Fassung.
  • Option B: The Comps-like Option (Great practice for written and oral senior comps!)
    • Prüfung: An in-class essay on a test or topic chosen by me, but which you will be able to read ahead of time.
    • Referat: Part of a one-on-one final oral exam we do at the end of the semester.
  • Option C (Only for the Dramen Unit): The Actor's Option
    • Prüfung: You and a partner write an abridged, contemporized version of one of the plays we read in class.
    • Referat: You and your partner perform your play either in person or record yourselves doing so.
Prüfungen. Writing is a process! For each Prüfung, you will likely submit multiple drafts:
  • Volle Fassung, You provide a Volle Fassung (Full Draft), which will represent your best effort at a polished writing, and will be graded for substance, effective communication, and demonstration of effective mechanics and vocabulary that you have learned in classes leading up to this one. For Option B, this will be your in-class essay.
  • Korrekturen. If needed, you will revise your Volle Fassung to resolve its issues.
    • You are allowed to submit as many Korrekturen as you want within seven cumulative days.
    • For each Korrektur, you can earn up to 100-10*n % of the points you missed on your previous draft, e.g. on your first Korrektur 90% of your Volle Fassung, on your second Korrektur 80% of your first Korrektur, etc.

Cultural Enrichment (5%)

Over the semester, you will participate in a certain number of cultural enrichment activities related to German-speaking culture outside of class, like watching a movie or attending a German Club meeting. See this list for possible activities.

You will submit a short report on the activity (in German). The number of necessary activities will depend on the amount of effort required, as well as when you submit your report (submissions in May count less than those in December and January).
