Course Description and Objectives

In GERM 1001, you will develop fundamental language skills and learn about the history, culture, and everyday life of German-speaking countries, which will help you discover your own culture and language and make cross-cultural comparisons.

People in not only Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein, but also parts of Italy, Belgium, the Netherlands, France, Eastern Europe, the U.S., and Latin America speak German. Good pronunciation of German is an asset for voice majors and knowing German will help you understand the works and ideas of many who formed our world. Knowing more about foreign cultures reveals the assumptions we make about our own society.

After completing GERM 1001, your linguistic proficiency should be displaying novice-low or even novice-mid proficiency on the ACTFL scale or A1 on the Common European Framework of References for Languages scale.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to

  1. communicate and exchange information on familiar topics — including everyday activities, ordering food at a restaurant, shopping for groceries and clothing, and playing various sports — using phrases and simple sentences, sometimes supported by memorized language
  2. handle short social interactions in everyday situations by asking and answering simple questions
  3. write short messages and notes on familiar topics related to everyday life
  4. recognize pieces of information, words, phrases, and simple sentences related to everyday life in spoken German and sometimes understand the main topic of what is being said
  5. understand familiar words, phrases, and sentences in short and simple texts related to everyday life
  6. sometimes understand the main idea of a more difficult text you have read

Supplementary Skills

In the process of teaching the above objectives, this course will also nudge students to develop best practice aptitudes vital to continuing success in both academic and future settings. Such skills include:

  1. time management discipline, including meeting deadlines and intermediate term planning
  2. self-reflection related for instance to study habits and self-correction
  3. active learning and recognizing good faith effort
  4. self-driven exploration and sociability that conjoins experiences inside and outside the classroom
  5. media literacy in reference to the measured use of appropriate tools

Homework (25%)

You will complete a set of homework assignments for nearly every class period, so check Canvas every day. Expect to spend up to 2 hours on homework and preparation for each class session.

There is no partial credit for assignment sets. To get credit, you must do all of the following:

  • Complete the assignment set on time, even if you will be absent that day.
  • Complete all parts of the assignment set. Every single blank of every single exercise must be filled.
  • Make a good faith effort to complete exercises as they are intended. For instance, if an exercise asks for a complete sentence, you provide a complete sentence and not a one-word answer.
  • Reattempt exercises at least once if the autograder shows them as incorrect.

A missed or incomplete assignment set can only be made up after you get full credit on three assignment sets in a row. You must then submit the missed assignment set by the next class period.

Quizzes (25%)

Every third class period will have a short quiz on vocabulary and grammar. Each quiz assumes continued mastery of previous topics.

  • Corrections. You can earn back up to 50% of the points you lost on a quiz by resubmitting your quiz with corrections within two class periods of receiving it back.
  • Missed quizzes can be taken in the next office hour with Dr. van der Kolk.

Projects (22%, 11% each)

You will complete two projects that showcase your communicative skills (reading, writing, listening, speaking, and culture) in German. Missed submissions will not be accepted.

Cultural Engagement (3%)

Over the semester, you will participate in activities outside of class related to German-speaking culture, such as a film viewing, a talk by a speaker, or a German Club chat hour. Elligible activities will be announced at the beginning of the semester. You will submit a report for each of the three events through Canvas.

  • Pronouns and Preferred Names. Let me know if you have a preferred name that differs from your legal name listed in the university's records, or if you have specific pronouns that you would prefer.

Academic Integrity

Aside from university policy, note the allowed and disallowed behaviors specific to our class:

Acceptable. DO THIS! Unacceptable. DON'T DO THIS!
Show off what you've learned on a project by using the expressions and grammar we have focused on. Use grammar and vocabulary or vocabulary that we haven't focused on. I will assume you got them from illicit sources, like an online translator or generative AI service.
Adapt an expression you saw in Impuls Deutsch for your own work. Copy out whole sentences from another source and claim it as your own work.
Ask someone to underline, but not correct, the mistakes in your project. Have a native speaker, friend, teammate, peer, or parent write corrections to your project.
Look up vocabulary words in Impuls Deutsch, Quizlet, or LEO to use in your project. Use an online translator to translate a sentence or paragraph for your project.
Paraphrase an outside source and use a citation. Use outside sources without citing.
Use Impuls Deutsch and your notes to check your genders, spellings, and plural forms. Have a grammar checker identify and revise your mistakes for you.
