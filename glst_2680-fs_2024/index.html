Course Description and Objectives

Why do fairy tales continue to capture our imagination, even as adults? In this course we will explore our timeless fascination with the genre through the reading, viewing, and discussion of select fairy tales from the sixteenth century through Hollywood’s latest creations. In our study we will discuss topics central to a nuanced understanding of fairy tales and the societies in which they find resonance. These subjects include the fairy tale’s structure, its commentary on gender, its aesthetic of violence, its definition of beauty, and its depictions of the Other (e.g. Beauty and the Beast).

The course schedule is generally segmented according to theme. This structure allows us to contrast different cultures’ commentaries on a subject by examining thematically related texts. Our approach challenges us to hone our critical reading skills because we do not engage relevant literary theory and criticism until we have analyzed the primary texts ourselves. By so doing, we call upon ourselves to reflect on and critique various modes of understanding the stories’ layers (Freudian, Jungian, feminist, Marxist, etc.). We will read and discuss texts pertaining to these approaches as a class.

Humanities Competency

The humanities challenge students to critically examine their and others' beliefs and the beliefs on “human existence” or rather “what it is to exist as a human being.” Humanities courses address issues that have captured our attention for over 3000 years, including matters of value. Courses that address them require students to critically assess diverse understandings of life’s aesthetic, ethical and moral dimensions and require students to learn methods of critical analysis and to understand the value of knowledge. Humanities courses contribute to each student’s understanding of how to choose a life worth living.

The following program learning outcomes define the Humanities Competency.

Students who have completed the General Education Humanities requirements can:

  1. Distinguish artistic, literary, philosophical or religious creations from other types of work and describe how they address enduring human concerns and the human condition.
  2. Apply discipline-specific criteria and evaluate the significance of specific literary, artistic, philosophical or religious works to enduring human concerns and the human condition.
  3. Apply discipline-specific knowledge in the humanities to contrast their understanding with that of others of the significance of specific artistic, literary, philosophical or religious works to enduring human concerns and the human condition.

FORL 2680 Course-Specific Student Learning Outcomes

Students are expected to master the following General Education Humanities and Course-Specific student learning outcomes: (Humanities Competency = “HUC” and Course-Specific Competency = “CSC”). Students who have successfully completed this course are able to:

  1. Distinguish fairy tales from other literary and filmic texts in terms of content, structure, and style (HUC1).
  2. Explain the relevance of fairy tales to understanding human concerns and our shared human condition (HUC1).
  3. Apply literary critical analysis to illuminate and analyze ideas germane to understanding our shared human condition (HUC2).
  4. Apply knowledge gained from studying fairy tales to enduring human concerns (HUC3).
  5. Evaluate primary sources critically and develop and justify original theses about them (CSC1).
  6. Discuss the limits and potential of human perception and understanding (special attention on related Enlightenment and Romantic thought) (CSC2).
  7. Describe how the struggle of reason against the passions might be necessary to create order in human relationships and to pursue happiness (CSC3).

Reading Quizzes (25%)

You will have one reading comprehension quiz as part of each module (11 quizzes total). You will be allowed a maximum of 2 attempts on each quiz because mastery of the material is our goal.

Discussion Board Contributions (25%)

I expect you to contribute to discussion board discussions with me and the members of our class up to two times per week (8 total). The discussion boards build on each other and prepare you for the two-part research project later. You need to contribute to the discussions before their deadlines

Research Project (50%)

You will complete a research project in two installments over the course of the semester. This is not a group project unless you choose to work with a partner and get prior approval to do so from Dr. van der Kolk. The first part will be due near the middle of the term; the second part of the project will be due toward the end of the semester. Detailed information regarding the project will be posted on Canvas, along with a scoring rubric. The purpose of the project is to encourage you to connect our study of fairy tales (as literature) to our intellectual interests in and outside the Humanities.
