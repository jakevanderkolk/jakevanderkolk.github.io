Course Description and Objectives

Why do fairy tales still capture our imagination, even as adults? In this course we will explore our timeless fascination with the genre through the reading, viewing, and discussion of select fairy tales from the sixteenth century through Hollywood’s latest creations. In our study we will discuss topics central to a nuanced understanding of fairy tales and their societies. These subjects include fairy tales' structure, their commentary on gender, their aesthetic of violence, their definition of beauty, and their depictions of otherness.

The course schedule is generally segmented according to theme, allowing us to contrast different cultures’ commentaries on a subject by examining thematically related texts. After reading our texts, we will reflect on and critique various modes of understanding (Freudian, Jungian, feminist, Marxist, etc.) as a class.

Humanities Competency

The humanities challenge students to critically examine their and others' beliefs, particularly on "what it is to exist as a human being." Humanities courses address issues that have captured our attention for over 3000 years, including life’s aesthetic, ethical, and moral dimensions. They require students to learn methods of critical analysis and to understand the value of knowledge. Humanities courses contribute to each student’s understanding of how to choose a life worth living.

The following program learning outcomes define the Humanities Competency.

Students who have completed the General Education Humanities requirements can:

  1. Distinguish artistic, literary, philosophical or religious creations from other types of work and describe how they address enduring human concerns and the human condition.
  2. Apply discipline-specific criteria and evaluate the significance of specific literary, artistic, philosophical or religious works to enduring human concerns and the human condition.
  3. Apply discipline-specific knowledge in the humanities to contrast their understanding with that of others of the significance of specific artistic, literary, philosophical or religious works to enduring human concerns and the human condition.

GLST 2680 Course-Specific Student Learning Outcomes

Students are expected to master the following General Education Humanities and Course-Specific student learning outcomes: (Humanities Competency = "HUC" and Course-Specific Competency = "CSC"). Students who have successfully completed this course are able to:

  1. Distinguish fairy tales from other literary and filmic texts in terms of content, structure, and style (HUC1).
  2. Explain the relevance of fairy tales to understanding human concerns and our shared human condition (HUC1).
  3. Apply literary critical analysis to illuminate and analyze ideas germane to understanding our shared human condition (HUC2).
  4. Apply knowledge gained from studying fairy tales to enduring human concerns (HUC3).
  5. Evaluate primary sources critically and develop and justify original theses about them (CSC1).
  6. Discuss the limits and potential of human perception and understanding (special attention on related Enlightenment and Romantic thought) (CSC2).
  7. Describe how the struggle of reason against the passions might be necessary to create order in human relationships and to pursue happiness (CSC3).
