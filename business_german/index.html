Course Description and Objectives

In this course, you will develop your language skills with an emphasis on commerical practices, business culture, and the economic sphere of German-speaking countries. Aside from a bespoke grammar review, the course will focus on furthering students' reading, writing, lexical, and discursive tactics for use in a foreign-speaking business environment. Students who successfully complete this course will meet or exceed the C1 or lower advanced competency level.

Our course will include:

  • in-class activities, including pair and group work focusing on target content. Active participation is essential
  • daily homework, including grammar and vocabulary practice. You will review grammar and vocabulary predominately outside of class.
  • contemporary readings related to commerce and professional development
  • group and individual projects where you implement your novel skills in a realistic context.

    Participation (die Beteiligung, 12,5%)

    For each class, you will receive up to 2 points for your participation grade.

    • Be Present. Please let Dr. van der Kolk know if you will be absent for a class session. Missing a class will give you a zero for that session. However:
      • You can earn back get full credit (2 points) for a missed session by completing a Supplementary Assignment noted in that day's homework. Submit them within 48 hours of your missed session or the end of your college-sanctioned event (whichever is later).

      To get 2 points for normal attendance, you must accomplish all of the following

      • Be On Time. You are no more than 5 minutes late.
      • Engage. You involve yourself in all class meetings, including group and whole-class activities, and make a good faith effort to express yourself in German. However:
        • Linguistic Correctness. You are not expected to speak perfectly, just to try your best as often as you can with the language you have learned!
      • Be prepared. You come to class ready and willing to practice the topics touched on in your homework and previous class sessions. You have all necessary materials (books, worksheets, notes, etc.) at hand.
      • Cooperate. You support and encourage your fellow students. Be an asset to others' learning, and they will be an asset to you.

    Homework (12,5%)

    Each day you should check the homework plan posted on Canvas. It is your responsibility to complete homework as assigned on a daily basis.

    Expect to spend 1-2 hours on homework and preparation for each class session. Please see me if you feel you are working more than this amount.

    There is no partial credit for individual assignments. To get full credit, you must do all of the following:
    • On Time. You submit the set of assignments on time, even if you will be absent that day.
    • Completion. You submit all parts of all assignments due that day. Every single blank of every single assignment must be filled in.
    • Present a good faith effort, that is, you try to genuinely engage with the material. It is not good faith to just copy-and-paste answers from the answer sheet or give a one word answer for a question asking for a complete sentence.

    Projects (die Projekte, 75% total, 15% for the first, second and third, 30% for the final)

    You will have four projects over the semester, two solo and two as group projects. Each will have a written and oral component.

    Criteria. Completing a project is process! For each one, you must submit multiple increments.
    • Vorschau, completion counts as 10% of project grade. You must complete in good faith and on time a short Vorschau (Preview). Each project will include a preliminary proposal of your project where you plan out what you intend to do.
    • Schriftliches Produkt, 50% of project grade. You provide your textual product, which will represent your best effort at a polished formal work, and will be graded for substance, effective communication, and demonstration of progress in learning mechanics and vocabulary that we have been learning in the course.
    • Korrekturen. If needed, you will revise your schriftliches Produkt to resolve its issues.
      • You are allowed to submit as many Korrekturen as you want before the provided due date.
      • For each Korrektur, you can earn up to 100-10*n % of the points you missed on your previous draft, e.g. on your first Korrektur 90% of your Volle Fassung, on your second Korrektur 80% of your first Korrektur, etc.
    • Vortrag, 40% of project grade. Each project will have an spoken component, e.g. an interview (Vorstellungsgespräch) or a pitch. You must adequately express yourself or your product, provide necessary materials, and show adequate linguistic correctness appropriate for the context.

    How to Succeed

    The most successful students typically do the following:

    • Exercise Self Care
      • Get your sleep! 7-9 hours (in a row).
      • Eat well and exercise. Manage your diet and get some physical activity every day.
      • Be mindful of substance use. A clear brain works and feels better.
      • Get involved. Make friends. Participate in sports, clubs, etc. Find your niche.
    • Get your money's worth:
      • Use office hours. Email me to make an appointment, or feel free to "stop by" with questions! Don't suffer from FMOOWMP! →
      • Talk a lot! Make lots of mistakes! Talking a lot instead of well is the best way to learn. You will make mistakes as you try out new vocabulary and grammar. This shows me how you've been learning.
      • Ask questions! When you’re lost, confused, or have a question, someone else probably has the same one (and they’ll be glad you asked).
      • Overwhelmed? Frustrated? Get help NOW! This course builds on itself, so any issue you have now will only get bigger. Save yourself aggravation and extra work further down the line by taking advantage of the extra videos I post, e-mailing me, or coming to my office hours.
    • Keep up. College language classes move fast and require sustained engagement. Students most often fail language courses because they fall behind. Make sure to:
      • Show up. Even with make-up assignments, missing one or (even worse) several sessions in a row makes everything you learn afterwards harder. Make things easier on yourself and be here when you can. Be absent as sparingly as possible!
      • Complete all assignments on time. Missing a day's worth of homework won't just cost you points, it will leave you unprepared for the next day's lesson and the next assignment.
    • Learn smarter, not longer. Studies show that people retain knowledge better and quicker when they learn small amounts at a time and review repeatedly. With a little foresight and planning, you can save time and frustration if you:
      • Build a routine. Set aside a time every day, every other day, or every few hours to do your work. Make it a habit.
      • Small tasks. Cramming is much less efficient than breaking assignments into a series of smaller, manageable tasks that allow your mind time to digest what it just learned.
      • Be opportunistic. Use time that you might waste (e.g. standing in a long line, eating by yourself, waiting for class to start) practicing vocabulary instead of viewing cat photos on your phone.
      • Take breaks. Give yourself time to let things sink in when studying. Schedule a break every so often so that you don't work for more than an hour at a time.
      • If you get stuck, revisit later. If a particular lesson or exercise frustrates you, put it aside for a while, work on something else for a while, and let your brain reset. It may make sense later.
      • Put in the time. Language learning isn't hard, but it does take time. We expect you to work up to 12 hours a week outside of class on coursework.
      • If you need more than 12 hours/week to keep up, or just feel overwhelmed, get help! Come and see me in my office hours, so that we can hash out methods that will work specifically for you.
    • Find study partners. Get to know your classmates and form groups in which you can collaborate on day-to-day assignments, and study for tests. But make sure you still do your own work!
    • Master the basics. We learn the most basic language first because it is the most important, so be patient. Before you can sprint, you need to know how to crawl.
      • Vocabulary, vocabulary, vocabulary! Having a strong working vocabulary makes everything easier and faster. Learn a few words every day. Use our Quizlet utilities or (even better) old-fashioned paper flash cards – find what works for you!
    • Make learning fun! Make class fun and welcoming for yourself and your classmates by participating in lighthearted yet respectful ways.
    • Celebrate your progress! You are doing something amazing: Over the next 15 weeks you will learn what took you years in your first language. Celebrate that every once in a while.

    Academic Integrity

    Note the following issues specific to our class:
    Acceptable. DO THIS! Unacceptable. DON'T DO THIS!
    Adapt an expression you saw in a reading for your own work. Copy out whole sentences from another source and claim it as your own work.
    Ask someone to underline, but not correct, the mistakes in your essay. Have a native speaker, friend, peer, or your mom provide corrections to your essay (effectively re-writing it for you).
    Look up vocabulary words in our vocabulary lists. Use an online translator to translate a sentence or paragraph.*
    Paraphrase an outside source and use a citation. Use outside sources without citing.
    Check your grammar assignment submission with the provided answer key after completing the assignment. Copy answers straight off of the answer key and submit them as your own.
    Use a textbook and your notes to check your genders, spellings, and plural forms. Use a grammar checker to identify and revise your mistakes.
    Use the expressions and grammar you have learned the best you can on a test. Use on a test grammar and vocabulary that we haven't learned (I will assume you got them from illicit sources).
    *Online translators haven't been the best at translating. A student, who was going to be absent in a course, once sent this email to a colleague named Rob:
    Lieber Fruchtsirup: Ich verpasse heute die Kategorie.

    English: Dear Fruit Syrup: I'm passing by the category today.†
    †She had entered "Dear Rob: I'm missing class today." in an online translator.
    Instead, rely first on the vocabulary you have learned and thus know well!
