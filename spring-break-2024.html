Overview

Want to knock out a (1) Cultural Enrichment credit (Kulturpunkt) over Spring Break? Well then choose one of the following activities! Submit a report for your chosen activity here (if you are in GERM-1001), here (if you are in GERM-1002), or here (if you are in GERM-2004) by class time on Monday, March 11 (the first day back from Spring Break).

Example Activities

Possible activities include:

Kulturelles Erlebnis

Experience an event related to German-speaking or foreign-language culture.

Dr. Rebecca Walkowitz talk "The New Multilingualism". If you haven't done so already, you can watch the talk advertised a couple weeks ago (Password: McMahon!24), and then submit a report on it.

Overhear German speakers in a store and have a short conversation with them in German for a couple minutes. Write a 240 word report in English.

Gossip with a friend, relative, brother in German for a half hour. Write a 240 word report in English.

Technologie auf Deutsch

Use technology that you interact with often to learn German! Examples:

Play a video game in German or with German subtitles on (turn on English subtitles if you want). Write a 240 word report about your experience and note any new words you may have learned. You should play for at least two hours long in German.

Watch a Twitch stream in German. If you go to "Browse" and put in "German" in next to "Filter by". "Just Chatting" sessions like from this streamer are often quite entertaining, and you'll make out a few new interesting words. Write a 240 word report about your experience and note any new words you may have learned. You should attend a stream for at least two hours cummulatively, and should occassionally attempt actively chatting with other participants.

Play a multiplayer video game with German-speakers in English or German (try a little bit) in chat/Discord/etc. with teammates for at least two hours. Write a 240 word sentence analysis in English about it.

Kino!

Watch a movie in German of at least 90 minutes long that you haven't seen before with German audio on (and either English subtitles or German and English subtitles. Use this Chrome extension to do so in Netflix). Write an 240 word report about what happened, your thoughts about the film, etc. Limit one per week. A few of my favorites include:

  • Die fetten Jahre sind vorbei. = The Edukators
  • Good Bye Lenin!
  • Das Leben der anderen = The Lives of Others
  • Gegen die Wand = Head On
  • Lola rennt = Run, Lola, Run!
  • Die Büchse der Pandora, 1929. Starring the manifestation of flapper-dom herself, Kansas native Louise Brooks, this film deals with a true femme fatale who leads many a man astray.
  • M, 1929. A wild hunt ensues in search of child murderer.
  • Sonnenallee, 1999. A humorous look at life in 1980s East Berlin.
  • Berlin, die Sinfonie der Großstadt, 1927. A documentary about life in Berlin in the 1920s.
  • Der blaue Engel, 1930. A stodgy school teacher becomes enamored with a cabaret singer.
  • Das Testament des Dr. Mabuse, 1933. The most critically acclaimed of the Mabuse franchise of films, which center on an evil genius and his sinister machinations.
  • Das Kabinet des Dr. Caligari, 1919. A psychological horror film classic from the silent era.
  • Metropolis, 1927. In a dystopian future, workers must live in the dark underground while the rich enjoy a futuristic city of splendor.
  • Der letzte Mann, 1924. An aging doorman's happiness crumbles when he is relieved of the duties and uniform which had for years been the foundation of his happiness and pride.
  • Anything else you might find on hand in Netflix. Here are a few lists of well-liked German films on Netflix.

Im Fernsehen gesehen: Streaming Services

Watch 120 minutes of a German TV show with German audio on (and either English subtitles or German and English subtitles. Use this Chrome extension to do so in Netflix). Write an 240 word report in English about what happened, your impressions, etc. Some of the hot German shows right now:

  • Dark (Netflix) A mind-bending science fiction film, often referred to as a German "Stranger Things," but a good bit more adult.
  • Deutschland 83 (and if you finish that, the next season Deutschland 86) (Hulu or Amazon Prime) A spy thriller following an East German in deep cover who inflitrates the West in 1983.
  • Babylon Berlin (Netflix) A police procedural/murder mystery that takes place in 1920s Berlin. Drugs, sex, and Rock and Roll Jazz!

Im Fernsehen gesehen: Direct Streams from German Broadcasters

See what's on right now (or on demand) directly through German broadcasters. Watch shows for two hours and write 240 words in English about what you saw, new words you learned, etc. Examples:

  • Schoener Fernsehen, where you can stream channels like ARD, ZDF, VOX, RBB, arte, etc. live.
  • Zweites Deutsches Fernsehen (ZDF) (free), one of the public broadcasters in Germany. You may be particularly interested in ZDF's heute Show, Germany's take on news comedy like The Daily Show and Last Week Tonight with John Oliver.
  • DANK's list of other public broadcasters, where you can find links to ARD, RTL, Deutsche Welle, etc.

Im Web gesehen: Webisodes for Language Learners

There are some good online resources geared for language learners. Watch 120 minutes of videos and summarize what you learned (e.g new words or cultural facts) and/or saw in 240 words in English. Examples:

  • The Lernen to Talk Show. One student's inspiring story of how he learned German in a year while in Germany. His weekly videos include subtitles, and show how he progresses and his amusing encounters with living abroad.
  • EasyGerman videos on YouTube. Interviews with people in the streets. The episodes on Karneval in Köln are especially funny.
  • Get Germanized on YouTube. A language teacher gives light-hearted tips and tricks about German language and culture.
  • The Telenovela Jojo sucht das Glück from Deutsche Welle. A short "soap opera" in simple German about a Brazilian woman who moves to Germany.
  • Extr@ (Episode 1), another telenovela about Sam, an American who moves to Germany, and his interactions with several roommates in a Berlin WG. Has easy German and German subtitles!
  • The German Department at the University of Michigan also has a huge list of ways to watch German online.
